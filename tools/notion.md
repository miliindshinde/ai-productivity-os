## What Is Notion?

Notion is a digital notebook that you can shape into almost anything — a to-do list, a project tracker, a knowledge base, or a simple daily journal. Think of it as a blank canvas where every page can hold text, tables, checkboxes, images, and links.

It is the **Memory** layer in the 3-Tool System because it is the best place to save the outputs from your AI tools so you don't lose them.

---

## Who Should Use It?

- Students who want one place for all their notes, assignments, and research
- Freelancers who need to track clients, projects, and deadlines
- Anyone who wants to save their best AI-generated answers and build on them over time

## The 3 Things You Will Actually Use

### 1. Pages
A page is just a document. You can write anything on it — notes from a meeting, a project plan, a list of ideas. Pages can contain other pages inside them, so you can build a folder-like structure.

### 2. Databases
A database in Notion is like a smart spreadsheet. You can use it to track your projects, clients, reading list, or habits. Each row in the database can open into its own full page with more detail.

### 3. Templates
Notion has hundreds of free templates you can copy in one click. Search "Notion templates" for options, or use these starter ideas:
- Weekly planner
- Project tracker
- Reading list
- Meeting notes

## How to Set Up in 30 Minutes

**Step 1:** Go to [notion.so](https://www.notion.so) and sign up with your Google account.

**Step 2:** Create your first page. Name it "My AI Workspace."

**Step 3:** Inside that page, create three sub-pages:
- **AI Outputs** — paste your best AI answers here
- **Projects** — one page per active project
- **Ideas** — a quick-capture inbox for anything interesting

**Step 4:** Bookmark Notion in your browser or download the mobile app so it is always one tap away.

---

## Using Notion With AI Tools

The best workflow is a two-step loop:

1. **Ask** your AI (Gemini, Claude, ChatGPT) a question or request a draft
2. **Save** the output into Notion so you can reuse and build on it

**Example:** Ask Claude to write an email template for following up with a client. Paste the final version into your Notion "Email Templates" page. Next time you need it, it is right there — no need to re-prompt.

## Quick Tips

- Use `/` on any Notion page to open the command menu and quickly add tables, checklists, or headings
- Press `@` to mention a date or another page as a link
- Use the **Web Clipper** browser extension to save any webpage directly into Notion
- The mobile app is excellent for quick captures on the go

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Over-organizing too early.** Start with 3 simple pages and only add structure as you need it.
- **Trying to move everything in at once.** Pick one workflow (e.g., saving AI outputs) and make it a habit before adding more.
- **Ignoring templates.** Notion's built-in templates save hours of setup time.
