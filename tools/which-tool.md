# 🤔 Which AI Tool Should I Use?

Not sure which AI brain to pick for your task? Use this guide to make the decision fast.

---

## Quick Decision Table

| Task | Best Tool | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Write an email or document | **Claude** | Produces natural, human-sounding writing |
| Get a fast, factual answer | **Perplexity** | Cites its sources so you can verify |
| Brainstorm ideas | **ChatGPT** | Creative and wide-ranging |
| Research a complex topic | **ChatGPT or Gemini** | Good at synthesizing information |
| Summarize your own documents | **NotebookLM** | Reads only what you upload |
| Get the latest news | **Grok or Perplexity** | Connected to live web/X data |
| Use it inside Word or Excel | **Microsoft Copilot** | Native Office integration |
| Use many AIs in one place | **Poe** | Lets you switch models freely |
| Talk through a problem casually | **Pi.ai** | Designed for conversation and support |
| Use free, open-source models | **HuggingChat** | Access to open models, no subscription |
| Everything Google (Docs, Drive) | **Gemini** | Deep integration with Google Workspace |
| Code help | **Claude or ChatGPT** | Both are strong; Claude is often cleaner |

## By Task Type

### Writing Tasks

**Best choice: Claude**
Claude produces the most natural, polished writing of any AI. It is the top choice for:
- Emails and messages
- Blog posts and articles
- Proposals and reports
- Anything that needs to sound like a real person wrote it

**Backup: ChatGPT or Gemini**
Both are capable writers. ChatGPT is more creative and willing to take risks. Gemini is tighter and cleaner for structured documents.

---

### Research & Fact-Finding

**Best choice: Perplexity**
Perplexity is built like a search engine — it gives you direct answers with links to the original sources. Use it when accuracy matters and you need to verify what you are told.

**Best for deep document research: NotebookLM**
NotebookLM reads your uploaded documents (PDFs, Google Docs, notes) and answers questions based only on those sources. It will not hallucinate facts from outside your files.

**Backup: ChatGPT or Gemini**
Both can summarize and explain topics well, but they do not always cite sources. Treat their answers as a starting point, not a final source.

---

### Brainstorming & Creative Work

**Best choice: ChatGPT**
ChatGPT is the most comfortable with creative, open-ended tasks. It generates more diverse ideas and takes more creative risks than other models.

**Backup: Claude**
Claude is a close second. It tends to produce more coherent and well-structured creative output.

---

### Real-Time & News Topics

**Best choice: Grok**
Grok has a live connection to X (formerly Twitter), making it ideal for trending topics, real-time news, and what people are saying right now.

**Backup: Perplexity**
Perplexity also searches the live web and is often better for factual news than social conversation.

---

### Quick Everyday Questions

**Best choice: Google Gemini**
Gemini works directly inside Google products (Gmail, Docs, Search) and is easy to reach for quick questions while you are already working in Google. It also performs well in the free tier.

---

### Emotional Support & Thinking Out Loud

**Best choice: Pi.ai**
Pi is specifically designed for back-and-forth conversation and emotional support. It asks follow-up questions, listens patiently, and helps you think through problems in a low-pressure way.

---

### Using Office Apps (Word, Excel, Teams)

**Best choice: Microsoft Copilot**
If your job uses Microsoft 365, Copilot is built directly into the apps you already use. It can summarize emails in Outlook, draft documents in Word, and analyze data in Excel without you leaving those tools.

---

## When the "Wrong" Tool Is Fine

Every major AI tool is capable enough to handle most tasks. If you are already using one tool and it is working, **do not switch just because this guide says something else is "best."** The best tool is the one you will actually use consistently.

The differences between Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini on most writing tasks are small. The habit of using AI every day matters far more than picking the perfect model.

---

## Free vs. Paid: What You Actually Need

Most users do not need to pay for any of these tools. The free tiers of Gemini, Claude, ChatGPT, and Perplexity are sufficient for daily personal use.

**Consider paying when:**
- You are hitting daily usage limits on tasks you do every day
- You need access to a specific paid feature (like generating images in ChatGPT Plus, or unlimited document uploads in NotebookLM Plus)
- You are using an AI tool professionally and billing it as a business expense

See individual tool guides for detailed pricing breakdowns.

## Which Memory Tool Should I Use?

### Quick Decision Table

| Tool | Best For | Free Tier | Learning Curve | Works Best With |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Notion** | Flexible notes, AI output saving, project pages | Unlimited pages | Low | Make.com, Zapier |
| **Airtable** | Structured tracking (clients, invoices, content) | 1,000 rows/base | Medium | Zapier, Make.com |
| **Obsidian** | Deep research, linked thinking, personal knowledge base | Fully free | Medium | Standalone |
| **Google Keep** | Quick mobile capture, simple sticky notes | Fully free | Very Low | Google ecosystem |

### By Use Case

**Best for general notes + saving AI outputs: Notion**
Notion is flexible enough to hold both structured data (tables, checklists) and unstructured writing (long notes, drafts). It is the best place to build a personal library of AI outputs, project pages, and running notes. Start here if you are unsure.

**Best for structured data and tracking: Airtable**
If your work involves tracking rows of data — clients, orders, content pieces, invoices — Airtable is more powerful than Notion for this. It has real database features like relational records, rollup formulas, and multiple views (grid, kanban, gallery). Choose Airtable over Notion when you need a spreadsheet that thinks.

**Best for connected research: Obsidian**
Obsidian stores notes as plain text files on your computer (no cloud dependency) and lets you link notes together into a knowledge web. It is the best choice for researchers, writers, or anyone building a long-term personal knowledge base. It has a steeper setup curve but is fully free.

**Best for quick capture on your phone: Google Keep**
When you just need to jot something down in 5 seconds on your phone and sync it to your computer, Google Keep is the fastest option. It is not for organizing complex projects — it is a digital sticky note.

## Which Automation Tool Should I Use?

### Quick Decision Table

| Tool | Free Plan | Visual Builder | Ease | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Zapier** | 100 tasks/mo | No | Easiest | Beginners, quick first automations |
| **Make.com** | 1,000 ops/mo | Yes | Medium | Power users, complex flows, better free tier |
| **IFTTT** | Unlimited (3 applets) | No | Very Easy | Simple phone/smart home rules |
| **n8n** | Self-hosted free | Yes | Hard | Developers, full control, no usage limits |

### By Use Case

**Best for beginners: Zapier**
Zapier has the simplest setup experience — pick a trigger, pick an action, done. It connects over 7,000 apps, more than any other tool. The downside is the free plan only allows 100 tasks per month and single-step workflows. Start here if you have never built an automation before.

**Best free plan + more power: Make.com**
Make.com gives you 1,000 operations per month for free (10x Zapier's free tier) and supports multi-step flows, filters, and routers at no cost. It uses a visual drag-and-drop canvas that makes complex workflows easier to understand. Choose Make.com if you expect to build more than one or two automations.

**Best for simple phone rules: IFTTT**
IFTTT ("If This, Then That") is the simplest tool for basic personal automations — like turning on smart lights when you arrive home, or saving your Instagram posts to Google Drive. It is not suitable for business workflows but is unbeatable for simple, personal triggers.

**Best for full control (advanced): n8n**
n8n is a self-hosted, open-source automation tool with no usage limits. It is the most powerful option but requires technical setup (running your own server or using their paid cloud). Only choose n8n if you are comfortable with technical tools and need to run automations at scale.
