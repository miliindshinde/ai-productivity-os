# 🛠️ The Master Tool List

Before we build any daily habits, you need to know what tools are available. Here is a master list of 50 tools, organized by what they do. You do not need all of them, but this is your menu to choose from.

## 🧠 AI Brains (Thinking & Writing)
1. [**ChatGPT**](https://chatgpt.com): Great for everyday questions, writing drafts, and coding help.
2. [**Google Gemini**](https://gemini.google.com): Excellent for writing, planning, and looking up up-to-date web answers.
3. [**Claude**](https://claude.ai): The best choice for writing that sounds like a natural, real person.
4. [**Perplexity**](https://www.perplexity.ai): A smart search engine that gives you direct answers with links to its sources.
5. [**NotebookLM**](https://notebooklm.google.com): Perfect for reading and summarizing your own uploaded notes and documents.
6. [**Pi**](https://pi.ai): A friendly AI designed to have back-and-forth conversations and help you talk through ideas.
7. [**Copilot**](https://copilot.microsoft.com): Microsoft's AI assistant that works well if you use Word or Excel.
8. [**Groq**](https://groq.com): Known for giving AI answers incredibly fast.
9. [**HuggingChat**](https://huggingface.co/chat): A free, community-built AI that is open for anyone to use.
10. [**Poe**](https://poe.com): An app that lets you use many different AI brains (like Claude and ChatGPT) in one place.

## 🗄️ Memory (Note-Taking & Organization)
11. [**Notion**](https://www.notion.so): A blank digital notebook where you can build your own pages, trackers, and folders.
12. [**Airtable**](https://airtable.com): A smart spreadsheet that helps you connect and organize different types of data.
13. [**Obsidian**](https://obsidian.md): A note app that links your ideas together like a web so you can see how things connect.
14. [**Google Keep**](https://keep.google.com): Good for very quick, colorful sticky notes on your phone or computer.
15. [**Apple Notes**](https://www.icloud.com/notes): The simple, built-in notes app for Apple users.
16. [**Evernote**](https://evernote.com): A classic tool for organizing notes, receipts, and clipped web pages.
17. [**Coda**](https://coda.io): Documents that act like mini-apps to help track projects.
18. [**Trello**](https://trello.com): A visual board where you move task cards from "To Do" to "Done".
19. [**Asana**](https://asana.com): A task tracker for organizing bigger projects with due dates.
20. [**Todoist**](https://todoist.com): A very simple, clean daily to-do list app.

## ⚙️ Automation (Connecting Your Apps)
21. [**Make.com**](https://www.make.com): A visual tool that connects your apps (like sending an email to a spreadsheet) automatically.
22. [**Zapier**](https://zapier.com): The most popular and easiest way to connect thousands of different apps.
23. [**IFTTT**](https://ifttt.com): Stands for "If This, Then That." Good for simple rules, like turning on smart lights.
24. [**Bardeen**](https://www.bardeen.ai): Lets you automate things right from your web browser with a few clicks.
25. [**n8n**](https://n8n.io): A free, advanced automation tool for people who want more control.
26. [**Relay.app**](https://www.relay.app): An automation tool built specifically to help teams work together.
27. [**TaskMagic**](https://www.taskmagic.com): Records where you click on your screen so it can repeat those clicks for you.
28. [**Apple Shortcuts**](https://support.apple.com/guide/shortcuts/welcome/ios): Built-in automation for iPhones and Macs.
29. [**Microsoft Power Automate**](https://make.powerautomate.com): Good for connecting Microsoft Office tools automatically.
30. [**Pipedream**](https://pipedream.com): A tool for connecting apps if you know a little bit of code.

## 🎨 Visuals (Presentations & Design)
31. [**Gamma**](https://gamma.app): Makes nice slide presentations and simple web pages in seconds using AI.
32. [**Canva**](https://www.canva.com): The easiest way to design social media posts, flyers, and logos without being a designer.
33. [**Midjourney**](https://www.midjourney.com): Creates highly detailed, beautiful pictures from your text descriptions.
34. [**DALL-E 3**](https://openai.com/dall-e-3): An easy picture-maker built right into ChatGPT.
35. [**Adobe Firefly**](https://firefly.adobe.com): A safe AI tool for adding or removing things from your photos.
36. [**Tome**](https://tome.app): Helps you tell stories by generating slides with text and pictures.
37. [**Figma**](https://www.figma.com): A tool for designing how websites and apps will look.
38. [**Miro**](https://miro.com): A giant digital whiteboard for drawing out ideas or planning out processes.
39. [**Napkin AI**](https://www.napkin.ai): Automatically turns your written text into neat diagrams and charts.
40. [**Beautiful.ai**](https://www.beautiful.ai): A presentation maker that automatically fixes the layout so your slides always look good.

## 🎬 Media (Video, Audio & Reading)
41. [**ElevenLabs**](https://elevenlabs.io): Makes highly realistic computer voices that sound like real humans.
42. [**Descript**](https://www.descript.com): Lets you edit a video just by deleting the typed words on a transcript.
43. [**CapCut**](https://www.capcut.com): A very popular and easy-to-use video editor for phone or computer.
44. [**HeyGen**](https://www.heygen.com): Creates videos of digital people talking based on the text you type.
45. [**Opus Clip**](https://www.opus.pro): Takes one long video and automatically chops it into short clips for social media.
46. [**Readwise**](https://readwise.io): Collects all the sentences you highlight in books and articles and puts them in one place.
47. [**Otter.ai**](https://otter.ai): Joins your online meetings to record the audio and type out notes for you.
48. [**Snipd**](https://www.snipd.com): A podcast app that lets you save and read summaries of your favorite episodes.
49. [**Feedly**](https://feedly.com): Gathers all your favorite news websites and blogs into one simple reading feed.
50. [**Pocket**](https://getpocket.com): Lets you save interesting articles or videos you find so you can look at them later.
